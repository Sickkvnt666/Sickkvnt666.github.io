Biography

Early Life and High School Career:

Peter Key was born and raised in Chicago, Illinois. He attended Lincoln Park High School, where he developed a passion for basketball at a young age. Key was a standout player on the school's varsity team, leading them to a state championship his senior year.

College Career:

After high school, he received a full scholarship to play for the University of Michigan Wolverines. As a freshman, Key made an immediate impact on the team, earning a starting spot and being named to the All-Freshman team in the Big Ten conference. He continued to improve each year, eventually being named a team captain and earning All-Conference honors.

Professional Career:

After completing his college career, Key was selected in the second round of the NBA draft by the Milwaukee Bucks. He spent his first few seasons with the Bucks, before being traded to the Phoenix Suns, where he played for five seasons. Key then signed with the Los Angeles Lakers as a free agent, where he played for the remainder of his career.

Throughout his time in the NBA, Key was known for his strong work ethic and ability to shoot from long range. He was a fan favorite wherever he played, and was named to the All-Star team twice during his career.

Post-Retirement:

After retiring from the NBA, Key became a basketball coach at his alma mater, Lincoln Park High School.
